System Overview

Intelligent Cardiovascular Monitoring for Athletes.

SafeBeat is a smart wearable monitoring system engineered to track real-time cardiovascular signals during physical activity. Powered by embedded sensors and an ESP32 processing unit, the system continuously analyzes heart rate patterns and blood oxygen levels to detect early warning signs of cardiac stress. Designed for athletes, SafeBeat transforms raw biometric data into actionable insights helping prevent risk before it becomes danger.

Unified Monitoring Ecosystem

Secure, real-time synchronization between the wearable vest, mobile interface, and web dashboard. Coaches and users can visualize live metrics, review historical data, and monitor performance from anywhere.

The Problem

The Most Dangerous Threat Is the One You Don't See.

Sudden cardiac events in young athletes are rare ,but when they occur, they are often unexpected. Most consumer fitness trackers measure surface-level metrics like heart rate, but they do not analyze deeper cardiovascular patterns or detect abnormal rhythm variations during intense physical stress. Without continuous signal monitoring, early warning signs can remain unnoticed. SafeBeat was created to bridge that gap providing intelligent, real-time analysis of heart rhythm patterns and oxygen saturation during performance.

High-Intensity Risk

Intense physical exertion increases cardiovascular strain, especially in undiagnosed conditions.

Real-Time Monitoring

Continuous signal analysis enables early detection of abnormal rhythm patterns during activity.

The Prototype

Engineered for Safety and Performance.

SafeBeat integrates medical monitoring sensors with a ESP32 microcontroller to acquire and process cardiovascular signals in real time. The system captures electrical heart activity while simultaneously monitoring oxygen saturation levels, enabling continuous analysis during physical exertion.

All hardware components are embedded inside a lightweight, impact-resistant module designed to attach seamlessly to compression apparel. Using optimized dry-contact electrodes and signal filtering algorithms, the prototype maintains stable readings even during high-intensity movement.
